# Conversation Patterns Skill

## Purpose

Provides systematic frameworks for conducting effective conversations, building rapport, facilitating discovery, and maintaining meaningful dialogue. Enables agents to guide conversations that uncover insights, establish trust, and create valuable interactions.

## When to Use This Skill

- Conducting initial discovery conversations
- Building rapport with new users
- Facilitating "get to know you" sessions
- Conducting interviews and information gathering
- Following up on previous conversations
- Deepening understanding of user needs and preferences
- Creating comfortable, productive dialogue environments

## Core Frameworks

### 1. Conversation Structure Framework

**Opening Phase (First 10-15% of conversation)**:
```
GREETING & CONTEXT:
  - Warm, personalized greeting
  - Acknowledge previous interactions if any
  - Set context for current conversation
  - Establish psychological safety

AGENDA SETTING:
  - State conversation purpose clearly
  - Set expectations for time and format
  - Invite user input on topics
  - Create collaborative framing

RAPPORT BUILDING:
  - Find common ground quickly
  - Use appropriate humor or warmth
  - Mirror communication style subtly
  - Demonstrate genuine interest
```

**Discovery Phase (Middle 60-70%)**:
```
STRUCTURED EXPLORATION:
  - Use question frameworks systematically
  - Balance breadth and depth
  - Follow conversational threads naturally
  - Create space for reflection

ACTIVE LISTENING:
  - Acknowledge what's being shared
  - Reflect back key points
  - Ask clarifying questions
  - Notice patterns and themes

DEPTH BUILDING:
  - Move from surface to substance
  - Explore motivations and values
  - Uncover underlying needs
  - Connect disparate topics
```

**Closing Phase (Final 15-20%)**:
```
SYNTHESIS & SUMMARY:
  - Recap key insights discovered
  - Highlight important themes
  - Connect learnings to outcomes

NEXT STEPS:
  - Define clear follow-up actions
  - Set expectations for continuity
  - Provide value-add resources
  - Leave door open for future dialogue

GRATITUDE & CLOSURE:
  - Express genuine appreciation
  - Reinforce relationship value
  - End on positive, forward-looking note
```

### 2. Question Framework Patterns

**The Funnel Approach**:
```
BROAD OPENING QUESTIONS:
  - "Tell me about your typical workday..."
  - "What's most important to you right now?"
  - "How do you currently approach [topic]?"
  - "What brought you here today?"

NARROWING QUESTIONS:
  - "When you mentioned X, what specifically do you mean?"
  - "Can you walk me through a recent example?"
  - "What happens when you try to [specific action]?"
  - "How often does this situation occur?"

DEEP DIVING QUESTIONS:
  - "Why is that important to you?"
  - "What would success look like?"
  - "What's been your experience with [specific detail]?"
  - "If you could change one thing, what would it be?"
```

**The SPIN Framework** (Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff):
```
SITUATION QUESTIONS:
  - Establish context and current state
  - "What's your current process for...?"
  - "How long have you been working on...?"
  - "Who's involved in...?"

PROBLEM QUESTIONS:
  - Uncover challenges and difficulties
  - "What challenges are you facing with...?"
  - "Where do you get stuck?"
  - "What's frustrating about the current approach?"

IMPLICATION QUESTIONS:
  - Explore consequences and impact
  - "How does this affect your workflow?"
  - "What's the cost of this problem?"
  - "What happens if this continues?"

NEED-PAYOFF QUESTIONS:
  - Focus on value of solutions
  - "What would it mean if you could...?"
  - "How would that improve your situation?"
  - "What benefits would you see from...?"
```

**The 5 Whys Technique**:
```
SURFACE STATEMENT:
  "I need better project management tools."

WHY #1: Why do you need better tools?
  "Because I can't track all my tasks effectively."

WHY #2: Why is tracking tasks difficult?
  "Because information is scattered across multiple systems."

WHY #3: Why is information scattered?
  "Because different teams use different tools."

WHY #4: Why do different teams use different tools?
  "Because there's no standard process for coordination."

WHY #5: Why is there no standard process?
  "Because we've grown quickly without formal structure."

ROOT INSIGHT: Need organizational process and coordination, not just tools.
```

### 3. Rapport Building Techniques

**Mirroring & Matching**:
```
COMMUNICATION STYLE:
  - Formal user → Professional language
  - Casual user → Relaxed, conversational tone
  - Technical user → Precise, detailed explanations
  - Creative user → Exploratory, open-ended dialogue

PACING:
  - Fast-paced user → Quick responses, efficient dialogue
  - Thoughtful user → Space for reflection, patient pauses
  - Detail-oriented → Thorough, comprehensive answers
  - Big-picture → High-level summaries with depth available

ENERGY LEVEL:
  - Enthusiastic → Match energy, show excitement
  - Reserved → Calm, measured responses
  - Analytical → Logical, structured approach
  - Emotional → Empathetic, validating tone
```

**Finding Common Ground**:
```
SHARED EXPERIENCES:
  - "Many people I work with face similar challenges..."
  - "I've noticed this pattern before in..."
  - "This reminds me of a common situation where..."

SHARED VALUES:
  - Identify what matters to the user
  - Reflect back their priorities
  - Align your approach with their values
  - Demonstrate understanding of their perspective

SHARED LANGUAGE:
  - Use terminology they use
  - Avoid jargon unless they use it
  - Adopt their metaphors and analogies
  - Speak their professional "dialect"
```

**Trust-Building Behaviors**:
```
CONSISTENCY:
  - Follow through on commitments
  - Maintain reliable patterns
  - Be predictably helpful
  - Honor boundaries and preferences

COMPETENCE:
  - Demonstrate expertise appropriately
  - Admit uncertainty when present
  - Provide accurate, thorough information
  - Show understanding of their domain

CARE:
  - Show genuine interest in their success
  - Remember personal details
  - Anticipate needs proactively
  - Prioritize their outcomes
```

### 4. Active Listening Framework

**Three Levels of Listening**:

**Level 1: Internal Listening** (Focus on yourself)
```
CHARACTERISTICS:
  - Thinking about your next question
  - Comparing to your experiences
  - Evaluating or judging
  - Planning your response

USE WHEN:
  - Need to process complex information
  - Making strategic decisions
  - Evaluating data objectively

AVOID WHEN:
  - Building rapport
  - Conducting discovery
  - Creating connection
```

**Level 2: Focused Listening** (Focus on speaker)
```
CHARACTERISTICS:
  - Full attention on what's being said
  - Notice words, tone, emphasis
  - Track logical flow
  - Catch key details

TECHNIQUES:
  - Summarize and reflect back
  - Ask clarifying questions
  - Note important phrases verbatim
  - Track themes and patterns

USE WHEN:
  - Gathering information
  - Building understanding
  - Solving problems together
```

**Level 3: Global Listening** (Focus on context and environment)
```
CHARACTERISTICS:
  - Notice what's NOT being said
  - Sense emotional undercurrents
  - Read between the lines
  - Detect patterns and themes

TECHNIQUES:
  - Notice shifts in energy or tone
  - Identify recurring topics
  - Sense hesitations or emphasis
  - Read contextual cues

USE WHEN:
  - Building deep understanding
  - Sensing unspoken needs
  - Navigating sensitive topics
```

**Reflection Techniques**:
```
PARAPHRASING:
  "So what I'm hearing is..."
  "It sounds like you're saying..."
  "Let me make sure I understand..."

SUMMARIZING:
  "If I could summarize the key points..."
  "What I'm taking away from this is..."
  "The main themes I'm hearing are..."

CLARIFYING:
  "Can you tell me more about...?"
  "What did you mean by...?"
  "Help me understand..."

VALIDATING:
  "That makes complete sense..."
  "I can see why that would be..."
  "It's understandable that you'd..."
```

### 5. Conversation Threading

**Following Threads Naturally**:
```
RECOGNIZE THREAD OPPORTUNITIES:
  - User mentions something in passing
  - Emotion or energy shifts
  - Repeated themes or topics
  - Interesting contradictions

DECIDE TO FOLLOW OR PARK:
  - FOLLOW: If relevant to conversation goals
  - FOLLOW: If emotional significance detected
  - PARK: If tangential but interesting
  - PARK: If time-constrained and less relevant

FOLLOWING A THREAD:
  "You mentioned [topic] earlier. I'm curious about that..."
  "I notice you keep coming back to [theme]..."
  "Tell me more about what you said about..."

PARKING A THREAD:
  "That's interesting - let's come back to that..."
  "I want to hear more about that later..."
  "Remind me to ask you about [topic]..."
```

**Creating Thread Continuity**:
```
WITHIN CONVERSATION:
  - Reference earlier points naturally
  - Connect disparate topics
  - Build on previous insights
  - Show you're tracking the narrative

ACROSS CONVERSATIONS:
  - Reference previous discussions
  - Follow up on past topics
  - Show continuity of understanding
  - Build longitudinal narrative
```

### 6. Icebreaker Techniques

**Low-Pressure Openers**:
```
WORK-RELATED:
  - "What's been keeping you busy lately?"
  - "What projects are you excited about?"
  - "What's a typical day like for you?"
  - "What's the best part of your current role?"

INTEREST-BASED:
  - "What do you enjoy doing outside of work?"
  - "What are you learning about these days?"
  - "What's been inspiring you recently?"
  - "What are you looking forward to?"

CHALLENGE-ORIENTED:
  - "What's the biggest challenge you're facing?"
  - "What would make your work easier?"
  - "What's frustrating you right now?"
  - "What are you trying to improve?"

ASPIRATIONAL:
  - "What are you working toward?"
  - "What would success look like for you?"
  - "What change are you hoping to create?"
  - "What's your ideal outcome?"
```

**Progressive Disclosure Framework**:
```
START SAFE (Surface Level):
  - Factual, non-threatening topics
  - Professional context
  - Observable information
  - Public knowledge

BUILD COMFORT (Personal but Professional):
  - Work preferences and styles
  - Professional challenges
  - Learning interests
  - Career aspirations

DEEPEN CONNECTION (More Personal):
  - Values and motivations
  - Personal goals and dreams
  - Significant experiences
  - Deeper challenges and concerns

ONLY IF APPROPRIATE (Deep Personal):
  - Sensitive topics
  - Vulnerable sharing
  - Personal struggles
  - Deep emotional content
```

### 7. Conversation Recovery Techniques

**When Conversation Stalls**:
```
ACKNOWLEDGE THE PAUSE:
  "Let me ask this a different way..."
  "That might not be the right question..."
  "Let me think about what's most important here..."

RESET WITH CONTEXT:
  "Let's step back for a moment..."
  "Before we go further, help me understand..."
  "I want to make sure we're covering what matters to you..."

SHIFT ANGLE:
  "Instead of [current approach], what if we talked about..."
  "Let me try a different angle..."
  "Maybe this will help - [new frame]..."

GIVE PERMISSION TO REDIRECT:
  "What would be most helpful to discuss?"
  "What should I be asking about instead?"
  "Where would you like to focus?"
```

**When You Misunderstand**:
```
ACKNOWLEDGE QUICKLY:
  "I think I misunderstood - let me try again..."
  "I got that wrong - can you clarify...?"
  "Help me understand what you actually meant..."

REFRAME WITHOUT DEFENSIVENESS:
  "So actually you're saying..."
  "What you meant was..."
  "Let me make sure I have this right now..."

LEARN FROM IT:
  "That's helpful - I see the distinction now..."
  "Thank you for clarifying..."
  "That makes much more sense..."
```

**When Conversation Gets Uncomfortable**:
```
ACKNOWLEDGE DISCOMFORT:
  "I sense this might be difficult to talk about..."
  "This seems like a challenging topic..."
  "I appreciate you being willing to discuss this..."

GIVE CONTROL:
  "We don't have to go there if you'd rather not..."
  "Tell me if this is too personal..."
  "You decide what you're comfortable sharing..."

REFRAME SUPPORTIVELY:
  "What I'm hearing is really important..."
  "This gives me insight into how to help better..."
  "Thank you for trusting me with this..."
```

### 8. Conversation Styles by Context

**Discovery Conversations**:
```
GOAL: Gather comprehensive information

APPROACH:
  - Open-ended questions dominate
  - Wide-ranging exploration
  - Follow interesting threads
  - Build holistic picture

BALANCE:
  - 80% listening, 20% talking
  - Questions > Statements
  - Curiosity > Solutions
  - Exploration > Conclusion
```

**Problem-Solving Conversations**:
```
GOAL: Understand and address specific challenges

APPROACH:
  - Structured problem definition
  - Root cause exploration
  - Solution brainstorming
  - Action planning

BALANCE:
  - 60% listening, 40% talking
  - Questions + Insights
  - Understanding + Guidance
  - Exploration + Direction
```

**Relationship-Building Conversations**:
```
GOAL: Establish trust and connection

APPROACH:
  - Personal sharing (appropriate)
  - Common ground discovery
  - Values alignment
  - Long-term thinking

BALANCE:
  - 70% listening, 30% talking
  - Interest > Information
  - Connection > Content
  - Rapport > Resolution
```

**Check-In Conversations**:
```
GOAL: Maintain continuity and catch updates

APPROACH:
  - Reference previous conversations
  - Follow up on past topics
  - Identify changes and updates
  - Surface new needs

BALANCE:
  - 60% listening, 40% talking
  - Continuity + Fresh Perspectives
  - Follow-up + New Discovery
  - Maintenance + Growth
```

## Best Practices

### For Building Rapport:
1. **Be Genuinely Curious**: Interest can't be faked effectively
2. **Match Their Energy**: But stay authentic to your character
3. **Remember Details**: Reference previous conversations naturally
4. **Show Respect**: For their time, expertise, and preferences
5. **Be Consistent**: Reliability builds trust over time

### For Effective Questioning:
1. **Ask One Question at a Time**: Avoid compound questions
2. **Use Open-Ended Questions**: Get richer responses
3. **Follow Natural Threads**: Don't rigidly follow a script
4. **Give Space for Thinking**: Silence is okay
5. **Ask "Why" Carefully**: It can sound judgmental - frame thoughtfully

### For Active Listening:
1. **Eliminate Internal Noise**: Focus fully on the speaker
2. **Resist Interrupting**: Let thoughts complete before responding
3. **Reflect Back**: Show understanding through paraphrasing
4. **Notice Patterns**: Track themes across the conversation
5. **Read Between Lines**: Pay attention to what's unsaid

### For Conversation Flow:
1. **Create Safety First**: People share when they feel safe
2. **Balance Structure and Flexibility**: Have a plan but adapt
3. **Read the Room**: Adjust based on responses
4. **Manage Time Wisely**: Respect boundaries while being thorough
5. **End with Clarity**: Clear next steps and follow-through
